# forhumans — Purpose & Mission

## What we believe

Software is built by technical people, for everyone else.

That gap — between the person who builds and the person who uses — is where most products fail. Not because the product doesn't work, but because it doesn't *communicate*. Features go undiscovered. Users feel lost. Trust erodes. People leave.

The fix is rarely more engineering. It's better human communication.

## What we're building

**forhumans** is a collection of Claude skills built around one principle:

> Software should make sense to the humans using it — not just the people who built it.

Every skill we publish closes the gap between builder and user. We translate system logic into human language. We replace jargon with clarity. We turn interfaces that assume knowledge into ones that build confidence.

## Who this is for

- **Solo founders** shipping fast without a UX writer or copywriter on the team
- **Small product teams** who know their product inside out but struggle to explain it to anyone else
- **Developers** who want their work to feel as good as it functions
- **Anyone** who believes that how something is communicated is as important as how it works

## What human-centric means

**Tech-centric** products describe what the system does.
**Human-centric** products describe what the human gets.

| Tech-centric | Human-centric |
|--------------|---------------|
| Describes mechanics | Describes outcomes |
| Uses the builder's vocabulary | Uses the user's vocabulary |
| Assumes context | Provides context |
| Labels features | Explains value |
| Silent where it seems obvious | Explains even the obvious |

Every skill in this repo applies this lens — to copy, to onboarding, to errors, to empty states, to every word a user reads.

## What we are not

We are not a generic prompt library.
We are not a one-size-fits-all writing tool.
We are not built for developers writing for developers.

We are skills with a point of view — opinionated, specific, and always on the side of the person using the product.

## How we grow

Every skill we add answers one question:
*"Where do products consistently fail the humans using them?"*

Current answer: **messaging and UX copy** — the words inside products that explain, guide, reassure, and direct.

Future answers: onboarding flows, data presentation, accessibility, error recovery, pricing communication, and more.

## The standard

Every skill published under forhumans must:

- Be built around the human using the product, not the system behind it
- Work across products, teams, and industries — not just one app
- Have a clear philosophy, not just a list of rules
- Make things simpler, clearer, and more human than before

If it doesn't serve the human on the other end — it doesn't belong here.
